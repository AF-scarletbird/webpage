001: Minesweeper

Locate some mines in the grid. To help you do so, some cells have been clued with numbers; these numbers indicate the amount of mines present in the immediate surroundings (the eight cells horizontally, vertically, or diagonally adjacent to the numbered cell).
Some cells are clued with a question mark (?). Like other clue cells, these cells do not contain. However, it is up to you to determine both where and how many mines are placed around these clues.

Other People's Puzzles

Below is a running list of (just some) of the puzzles that really stood out to me as examples of just what logic puzzles have to offer

  • 2026 WPF Puzzle GP Round 5, 10-11 : Ayeheya - This pair of puzzles showed me that there are a load of fun mechanism in this rotationally-symmetric variation of Heyawake. I was inspired to create my own (inferior) Ayeheya puzzle, which wound up being #006.
  • 2026 WPF Puzzle GP Round 3, 18-20 : Rail-Pool (complete liar) - despite an intimidating rule set at first glance, once you get to understanding the workings of the genre (as well as that of the vanilla Rail Pool), there are a wealth of clever deductions to be had here that really makes solving a satisfying experience.
